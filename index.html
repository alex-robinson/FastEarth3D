FastEarth3D

A fast, open-source, time-domain spectral–finite-element solid-Earth model

FastEarth3D is a state-of-the-art but deliberately simple and fast 3D solid-Earth model: a self-gravitating, incompressible, Maxwell-viscoelastic deformation model coupled to a self-consistent sea-level equation with migrating coastlines. It is designed as an open-source replacement for VILMA within the CLIMBER-X Earth system model (Willeit et al. 2022), and more broadly as a reusable glacial isostatic adjustment (GIA) engine for ice-sheet and sea-level work.

The numerical method is a clean-room reimplementation of the spectral–finite-element, time-domain approach of Martinec (2000): spherical harmonics horizontally, finite elements radially, and an explicit memory-stress integration of the Maxwell rheology in time. It is built 3D-ready from the start — laterally varying viscosity costs nothing extra in the solver — yet validated first in 1D against the published GIA community benchmarks (Spada et al. 2011; Martinec et al. 2018).

NoteA clean-room implementation

FastEarth3D reproduces the physics of VILMA — an incompressible, self-gravitating, Maxwell sphere with rotational feedback and a full migrating-coastline sea-level equation (Albrecht et al. 2024; Bagge et al. 2021) — entirely from the published literature. No VILMA source code is read or used. Where this documentation says “Martinec (2000), eq. (80)”, that equation was transcribed from the paper and re-derived independently.

Why this method

The decisive structural fact behind the method is that, with an explicit Maxwell time scheme, the stiffness solve has no angular coupling: each spherical-harmonic degree \(\ell\) decouples into an independent, small, banded radial system. This makes the solver embarrassingly parallel and cheap, and — crucially — makes lateral (3D) viscosity free: it never couples the harmonics in the solve, entering only through the explicitly-known memory stress, which is evaluated pointwise on the Gauss–Legendre grid and transformed back to spectral space each step (a pseudo-spectral treatment). The 1D and 3D code paths are therefore the same solver.

Method family Examples 3D viscosity Speed Fit for purpose
Normal-mode + Love numbers + SLE SELEN, TABOO, ALMA3 no (1D only) fast (1D) dead end for 3D
Time-domain spectral–FE VILMA (Martinec 2000) yes fast chosen
Full 3D FE/FV CitcomSVE, ASPECT, Elmer yes slow, heavy too complex

On top of the full time-domain solver, FastEarth3D adds an optional reduced modal solver — the per-degree relaxation collapsed onto a handful of exponential modes — as a faster, tunable approximation that converges back to the full solver. It is a dial, not a different method.

Status

The full model is implemented: the spectral–finite-element solver core, the viscoelastic time stepping and response operator, the self-consistent migrating-coastline sea-level equation, rotational feedback (polar motion), and laterally varying (3D) viscosity — together with restart and spin-up, online remapping of lon-lat forcing, and a host-coupling API. A second response operator, the reduced modal solver, offers a tunable, faster approximation that converges back to the full solver.

Validation against the Spada et al. (2011) and Martinec et al. (2018) community benchmarks covers the radial solver, the disc-load response, and the sea-level equation; rotation is checked against Spada test 3/2. Cross-code validation of the 3D viscosity path and the modal-vs-VE accuracy/cost study are ongoing — see the validation ladder.
